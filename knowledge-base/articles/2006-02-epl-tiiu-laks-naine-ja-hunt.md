# ZUGA "Naine ja Hunt" uurib kultuuride erinevusi

**Eesti Päevaleht**
Published: February 26, 2006
Author: Tiiu Laks

Theatre review of ZUGA ühendatud tantsijate contemporary dance performance "Naine ja Hunt" at Kanuti Gildi SAAL blackbox.

## Source

https://epl.delfi.ee/artikkel/51033163/zuga-naine-ja-hunt-uurib-kultuuride-erinevusi

## Key Themes

**Cultural and generational differences:**

> "Kõik on erinevad, aga mõned on veel erinevamad. Kuidas siis üksteist mõista"

The review explores the performance's investigation of:

- Different nationalities and generations
- Cultural codes and understanding
- Similarities and differences
- Environmental change and adaptation to new places

**The central relationship:**

- Kaja Kann (Naine/Woman) - Estonian, young, fast-paced
- Bush Hartshorn (Hunt/Wolf) - British, slower, cautious
- "Pidurid peal" (brakes on) - different tempo of life
- Different languages, different ways of understanding
- Gender differences emphasized

## Review Highlights

### Creative Process

> "ZUGA-t on alati huvitanud loomeprotsess ise, nagu ka leitud paigad."

The performance emerged from:

- Week spent in forest at a farm
- Investigating relationships between two different nations and generations
- Cultural codes, understanding, similarities and differences
- Impact of environmental change

### Multimedia Approach

**"Pildikesed Jõeperest"** (Little pictures from Jõepere)

- Video imagery used to solve presentation challenge
- Situations and searches roll before viewer on white screen
- Video shows both everyday situations and staged presentations
- Language used boldly in both video and live performance
- Dominant tone: **humor**

### Choreographic Quality

**Choreographer: Tiina Mölder**

- Clear line, clean, well-thought-out
- Creates sufficient connections but not too rigid
- **Contrasts work perfectly:**
  - One moves fast, the other slowly
  - Character differences clear
  - Similarities also found

### Design Elements

**Kalle Tikas** - Stage design, lighting, and original music

- Music inspired by Estonian folk dance **"Targa rehealune"**
- Integrated visual and sonic environment

### ZUGA's Approach

> "ZUGA jätab vaataja ettekujutusele ja arusaamadele alati palju ruumi"

- Leaves much space for viewer's imagination and understanding
- Opportunity to interpret as pleases most or as own experience determines
- Viewers can draw their own parallels

## Critical Context

**Opening metaphor** about modern life's pace:

- Person crawls their winding path in fast world
- Tries to climb upward, runs, rushes headlong
- Very hurried, must always be doing something
- Time is deficit, must fill all time units with meaningful activity
- Stress, tension, brain explosion
- Need to take time out - go to countryside

This frames the performance as exploring what happens when fast-paced modern life encounters different rhythms and cultures.

## Significance

- **International collaboration** - Estonian-British artistic partnership
- **Cultural exploration** - Not just dance, but investigation of communication across differences
- **Multimedia integration** - Video and live performance combined
- **ZUGA's methodology** - Process-based, site-specific creation
- **Accessibility** - Humorous tone makes complex themes approachable
